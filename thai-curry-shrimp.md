# Thai Curry Shrimp

*With Japanese Eggplant, Crispy Shallots & Cucumber Vinegar Salad*

---

## Overview

A rich, fragrant Thai curry built around plump shrimp and — when you can find them — slender Japanese eggplant, which soak up the coconut curry beautifully without turning mushy. Two simple garnishes do a lot of work here: crispy shallots add crunch and a savory depth, while the cold cucumber salad cuts right through the richness of the coconut milk with clean acidity.

**Serves:** 2–3
**Active time:** ~45 minutes

---

## Components

- Thai Curry Shrimp & Eggplant
- Crispy Shallots
- Cucumber Vinegar Salad
- Steamed jasmine rice (for serving)

---

## Cucumber Vinegar Salad

*Make this first — it benefits from time in the fridge while everything else comes together.*

### Ingredients

- 1 English or 2 Persian cucumbers (or use Japanese cucumbers if available)
- 2 tablespoons rice wine vinegar
- 1 teaspoon granulated sugar
- ½ teaspoon kosher salt
- Optional: a few thin slices of fresh red chili

### Method

1. Halve the cucumber lengthwise and use a spoon to scoop out and discard the seeds.
2. Dice the cucumber into roughly ½-inch pieces.
3. In a bowl, whisk together the vinegar, sugar, and salt until the sugar dissolves.
4. Add the cucumber (and chili if using), toss to coat, and refrigerate uncovered until ready to serve.

> The salad should be served cold. The vinegar and sugar ratio here is intentionally restrained — you want acidity, not a pickle.

## Crispy Shallots

### Ingredients

- 3–4 large shallots, peeled and thinly sliced into rings
- Neutral oil (vegetable, canola, or avocado), enough to shallow-fry (about ½ cup)
- Kosher salt

### Method

1. Slice the shallots as uniformly thin as possible — a mandoline helps a lot here.
2. Spread the rings out on a paper towel and press gently to dry. Any excess moisture will cause splattering and uneven crisping.
3. Heat the oil in a small saucepan or skillet over medium heat. Test readiness by dipping a shallot ring — it should sizzle immediately.
4. Add the shallots and cook, stirring frequently, until they turn deep golden brown. Watch carefully near the end; they go from golden to burnt quickly.
5. Scoop out with a slotted spoon and spread on a paper towel. Season lightly with salt.
6. They will crisp up further as they cool. Use within an hour or two for best texture.

> Save the shallot oil — it's phenomenal drizzled over rice or used in a dressing.

## Thai Curry Shrimp & Eggplant

### Ingredients

**Shrimp & Eggplant**
- 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 2–3 Japanese eggplants (when available), cut into 1-inch rounds or on the bias

**Curry Base**
- 2–3 tablespoons red or green Thai curry paste (adjust to your heat preference)
- 1 can (13.5 oz) full-fat coconut milk
- 1 tablespoon fish sauce, plus more to taste
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- Zest and juice of ½ a lime
- 1 tablespoon neutral oil

**Aromatics & Finishing**
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1-inch knob fresh ginger, grated
- 2–3 kaffir lime leaves (fresh or frozen), center vein removed, thinly sliced — optional but recommended
- A handful of fresh Thai basil leaves
- Lime wedges for serving

### Method

**1. Cook the eggplant.**
Heat the oil in a wide skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the eggplant in a single layer and cook, undisturbed, for 2–3 minutes until golden on one side. Flip and cook another 2 minutes. The eggplant doesn't need to be fully cooked through — it will finish in the curry. Remove and set aside.

**2. Bloom the curry paste.**
Lower the heat to medium. Add a small splash more oil if needed, then add the curry paste and cook, stirring constantly, for 1–2 minutes until fragrant and darkened slightly. Add the garlic and ginger and cook another 30 seconds.

**3. Build the sauce.**
Pour in the coconut milk, stirring to fully incorporate the paste. Add the fish sauce, sugar, and kaffir lime leaves if using. Bring to a gentle simmer and let cook for 5 minutes to meld the flavors.

**4. Add the eggplant back.**
Return the eggplant to the pan and simmer for another 4–5 minutes until just tender and fully cooked through.

**5. Add the shrimp.**
Nestle the shrimp into the curry in a single layer. Cook for 2–3 minutes, flipping once, until they're just pink and opaque. Don't overcook — shrimp carry over heat fast.

**6. Finish.**
Taste the curry and adjust — more fish sauce for salt and umami, a squeeze of lime for brightness, a pinch of sugar if it needs balance. Stir in the Thai basil off the heat.

---

## To Serve

Spoon the curry over jasmine rice in a wide bowl. Top with a generous pile of crispy shallots. Serve the cold cucumber salad alongside (or tucked right into the bowl next to the rice). Add a lime wedge.

The contrast is the point: hot, rich curry — cold, bright cucumber — crunchy, savory shallots.
