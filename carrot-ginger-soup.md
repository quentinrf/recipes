# Simple Carrot Ginger Soup

*Serves 4 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 30 min*

---

## Ingredients

- 1 kg (about 6–7 medium) carrots, peeled and roughly chopped
- 1 medium onion, roughly chopped
- 3 garlic cloves, smashed
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated (or more to taste)
- 2 tbsp olive oil or butter
- 1 litre (4 cups) vegetable or chicken stock
- 1 can (400ml) coconut milk *(optional — adds creaminess and a subtle sweetness)*
- Salt and pepper to taste

### To finish
- Squeeze of fresh lime or lemon juice
- Drizzle of good olive oil or a swirl of cream
- Fresh cilantro or chives (optional)
- Pinch of chili flakes or a dash of hot sauce (optional)

---

## Instructions

### 1. Sauté the aromatics
In a large pot over medium heat, warm the olive oil or butter. Add the onion and a pinch of salt and cook for 5–6 minutes until soft and translucent. Add the garlic and ginger and cook for another 1–2 minutes until fragrant.

### 2. Add the carrots and stock
Add the chopped carrots and pour in the stock. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 20–25 minutes, until the carrots are completely tender and easy to pierce with a fork.

### 3. Blend
Remove from heat. Using an immersion blender (or carefully transferring in batches to a standing blender), blend until completely smooth. If using coconut milk, stir it in now and blend briefly to combine.

### 4. Season and serve
Taste and adjust salt, pepper, and ginger. Add a squeeze of lime or lemon to brighten everything up. Ladle into bowls and finish with a drizzle of olive oil or cream, and any toppings you like.

---

## Tips & Notes

- **More ginger:** If you love a strong ginger kick, grate in a little extra fresh ginger after blending and stir through — the raw ginger adds a sharper, more pronounced heat.
- **No coconut milk?** A splash of heavy cream works just as well, or leave it out entirely for a lighter, more brothy soup.
- **Make ahead:** This soup keeps well in the fridge for 3–4 days and freezes beautifully for up to 3 months.
- **Spice it up:** A pinch of ground cumin or coriander added with the garlic and ginger adds a lovely warmth.
