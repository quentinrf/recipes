# Cold Roasted Beet Salad with Burrata & Walnut Dijon Vinaigrette

*Serves 2–4 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 50–60 min | Chill: 30 min*

---

## Ingredients

### For the beets
- 4 medium beets (red or a mix of red and golden), scrubbed
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- Salt and pepper

### For the Dijon vinaigrette
- 1½ tsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 1 small shallot, finely minced
- 4 tbsp good quality extra virgin olive oil
- Salt and pepper to taste

### For the salad
- 1 ball of burrata (about 125g), at room temperature
- ½ cup walnuts, roughly chopped
- Small handful of fresh tarragon leaves
- Small handful of flat-leaf parsley, roughly torn
- Flaky sea salt (such as Maldon), for finishing
- Extra virgin olive oil, for drizzling

---

## Method

### 1. Roast the beets
Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Wrap each beet individually in foil with a drizzle of olive oil and a pinch of salt. Place on a baking sheet and roast for 50–60 minutes, until a knife slides through easily. Let cool slightly, then rub off the skins with a paper towel (wear gloves if using red beets). Cut into wedges or thick rounds.

### 2. Dress while warm
Whisk together the Dijon, red wine vinegar, lemon juice, and minced shallot. Slowly stream in the olive oil while whisking until emulsified. Season with salt and pepper. Toss the warm beets in about two-thirds of the vinaigrette while they're still warm — they'll absorb the flavors as they cool.

### 3. Toast the walnuts
In a dry skillet over medium heat, toast the walnuts for 3–4 minutes, stirring frequently, until fragrant and golden. Set aside to cool.

### 4. Chill
Refrigerate the dressed beets for at least 30 minutes (or up to a day ahead) until fully cold.

### 5. Assemble
Arrange the cold beets on a flat serving plate or platter. Tear the burrata open and place it in the center or in pieces over the beets, letting the creamy stracciatella spill out naturally. Scatter the toasted walnuts and fresh herbs (tarragon and parsley) over the top. Drizzle the remaining vinaigrette and a little extra olive oil over everything. Finish with a generous pinch of flaky sea salt.

---

## Tips & Notes

- **Make ahead:** The roasted and dressed beets keep in the fridge for up to 2 days. Assemble with the burrata just before serving.
- **Mix up the beets:** A mix of red and golden beets looks beautiful and adds visual contrast on the plate.
- **Herb variations:** Chives work beautifully in place of tarragon if that's what you have on hand.
- **Extra richness:** A light drizzle of honey over the finished salad adds a lovely subtle sweetness that bridges the earthy beets and tangy vinaigrette.
