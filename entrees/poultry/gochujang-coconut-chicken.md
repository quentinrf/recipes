# Gochujang Coconut Chicken

*Lacquered chicken thighs over a silky, fermented-heat coconut broth — the kind of bowl that looks effortless and tastes like you've been cooking for hours. Sweet, spicy, deeply savoury, and finished with enough acid to cut right through the richness.*

**Serves:** 2
**Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 35 min | **Total:** 55 min (plus 20 min marinating)

---

## Components

1. **Chicken marinade / glaze** — marinate first, glaze last
2. **Gochujang coconut broth** — the heart of the dish
3. **Sticky rice dome** — the base
4. **Soy-marinated egg** — optional but worth it
5. **Garnishes** — bok choy, crispy onions, spring onion, sesame, lime, chilli oil

**Order of operations:** Start the egg first if making it (needs 30+ min in soy). Marinate the chicken. Build the broth. Cook the chicken and glaze. Char the bok choy. Assemble.

---

## Ingredients

### Chicken
- 2 boneless, skin-on chicken thighs
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp honey
- 1 tsp chilli crunch oil
- Neutral oil, for searing

### Gochujang Coconut Broth
- 1 red onion, finely sliced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 20g fresh ginger, peeled and finely grated
- 2 tbsp gochujang paste *(buy the real thing — a good Korean brand like Haechandle or Bibigo)*
- 1.5 tbsp honey
- 500ml good chicken stock *(homemade or a quality low-sodium store-bought — this is the backbone)*
- 1 x 400ml tin full-fat coconut milk
- 2 tsp fish sauce, plus more to finish
- Juice of 1 lime, to finish
- 1 tsp toasted sesame oil, to finish

### Soy-Marinated Eggs (optional but recommended)
- 2 eggs
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp rice vinegar
- 50ml water

### To Serve
- 300g cooked sticky (sushi) rice, hot
- 2 heads bok choy, halved lengthways
- 2 spring onions, finely sliced on the bias
- Crispy fried onions or shallots
- Chilli oil
- 1 lime, cut into wedges
- Sesame seeds (toasted)

---

## Method

### Soy-Marinated Eggs (start 30+ minutes ahead)
Bring a small pot of water to a rolling boil. Lower the eggs in gently and cook for exactly 6 minutes. Transfer immediately to an ice bath for 5 minutes, then peel carefully.

Whisk together soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and water in a small container just large enough to hold the eggs. Submerge the eggs and leave to marinate for at least 20 minutes, turning once. They'll take on a beautiful amber glaze. Slice in half lengthways to serve.

### Marinate the Chicken
In a bowl, combine soy sauce, honey, and chilli crunch oil. Add the chicken thighs and turn to coat thoroughly. Leave to marinate for at least 20 minutes at room temperature — don't skip this, it's doing double duty as flavour builder and glaze.

### Build the Broth
Heat a generous splash of neutral oil in a heavy-based saucepan over medium-high heat. Add the sliced onion with a pinch of salt and cook for 5–6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until completely soft and beginning to colour at the edges.

Add the garlic and ginger. Cook for 2 minutes, stirring, until fragrant and the raw edge is gone.

**Critical step:** Turn the heat down to medium. Add the gochujang paste and cook for a full 2–3 minutes, stirring constantly. The paste will darken slightly, smell almost nutty, and begin to caramelize against the pan. This blooming step is what separates a flat, one-dimensional broth from something with real depth — do not rush it.

Add the honey and stir for 30 seconds more.

Pour in the chicken stock and coconut milk. Stir to combine and bring to a gentle simmer. Add the fish sauce. Simmer uncovered for 15 minutes to allow the flavours to develop and the broth to concentrate slightly. Taste: it should be rich, spicy, and lightly sweet with a savoury backbone. Adjust fish sauce if it needs more depth.

**Do not finish with acid yet** — that comes at the end.

### Cook and Glaze the Chicken
Heat a heavy frying pan (cast iron if you have it) over medium-high heat until smoking. Add a thin film of neutral oil.

Place the chicken thighs skin-side down. Press gently with a spatula to ensure full contact. Cook undisturbed for 6–7 minutes — you're looking for deeply golden, crispy skin with rendered fat. Do not rush this; the skin sets the whole dish. Flip and cook for a further 4–5 minutes until cooked through (internal temp 75°C / 165°F).

Remove the chicken from the pan and pour off any excess fat, leaving about 1 tsp behind.

Add the reserved marinade to the hot pan. It will bubble and spit — let it reduce for 1–2 minutes until it thickens into a sticky, glossy glaze. Return the chicken to the pan and flip 2–3 times to coat fully. The glaze should cling and lacquer the surface. Remove from heat.

**Rest the chicken for 3 minutes** before slicing. This is non-negotiable — slice too early and you lose the juices you just worked to keep.

### Char the Bok Choy
In the same pan (don't clean it — those sticky bits are flavour), add the bok choy cut-side down over high heat. Cook for 60–90 seconds until lightly charred and just wilted. You want colour and a little smoke, not a full cook-through — they should still have bite.

### Finish the Broth
Just before serving, stir in the lime juice and sesame oil. Taste and adjust — the acid should lift the whole thing and balance the coconut richness. Add more fish sauce if it needs salinity. This finishing step is what makes the difference between good and exceptional.

---

## Assembly / To Serve

Pack hot sticky rice firmly into a small bowl, then invert it into the centre of a wide, shallow serving bowl to create a rice dome.

Ladle the hot broth around and partially over the rice dome — generous, not stingy.

Slice the rested chicken thighs on the bias into 4–5 pieces and arrange over the rice. Lay the bok choy halves alongside. If using, place the soy egg halves in the bowl, cut-side up.

Finish with: a pile of crispy fried onions over the chicken, a scatter of spring onion and sesame seeds, a drizzle of chilli oil, and a lime wedge on the side.

Serve immediately.

---

## Tips & Notes

**Chicken thighs, not breast:** The original recipe calls for breast, which will dry out under this treatment. Boneless thigh gives you far more flavour, better fat content, and a far more forgiving cook window. Skin-on is worth it for the crispy skin against the silky broth.

**Gochujang quality matters:** The fermented depth you're looking for comes from well-made paste, not cheap supermarket substitutes. Look for Korean-branded gochujang in a tub, not a squeeze bottle.

**Make ahead:** The broth can be made a day ahead and reheated — it actually improves overnight. The soy eggs can marinate for up to 24 hours (and get better for it). Cook the chicken and rice fresh.

**Heat level:** The broth is medium-spicy. Increase gochujang to 3 tbsp if you want more heat, or reduce to 1 tbsp for a milder version.

**Wine pairing:** An off-dry German Riesling (Spätlese) handles the heat and coconut beautifully. Alternatively, a cold Sapporo or Hite lager — don't overthink it.
