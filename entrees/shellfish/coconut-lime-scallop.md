# Coconut Lime Scallop
*Serves 4*

---

## Core Components

1. Sushi Rice Cakes (lightly fried)
2. Coconut Lemongrass Velouté
3. Cilantro, Thai Basil & Mint Vinaigrette
4. Seared Diver Scallops
5. Fried Shallots

## Optional Enhancements

- Yuzu Gel Dots *(visual & acidic element)*
- Pickled Watermelon Radish *(colour contrast)*
- Coconut-Braised Pork Belly *(or alternative proteins)*

---

## Sushi Rice Cakes

### Ingredients
- 2 cups sushi rice
- 2½ cups water
- 3 tbsp rice wine vinegar
- 1½ tbsp sugar
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- Neutral oil (grapeseed or canola) for frying

### Method
Rinse rice until water runs clear, then cook in a rice cooker or covered pot. While hot, fold in vinegar, sugar, and salt gently — don't mash. Transfer to a parchment-lined 8×8 pan, press firmly with another pan weighed down on top, and refrigerate uncovered at least 8 hours or overnight.

Cut into rectangles roughly 1×2 inches. Heat a thin film of neutral oil in a heavy skillet over medium heat. Fry rice pieces 1½–2 minutes per side until lightly golden and just beginning to colour — you want a soft crust that will absorb the sauce rather than repel it. The interior should stay moist. Transfer to a wire rack and season lightly with salt.

---

## Coconut Lemongrass Velouté

### Ingredients
- 2 tbsp unsalted butter
- 2 leeks, white and light green parts only, thinly sliced and washed
- 2 stalks lemongrass, outer leaves removed, bruised with the back of a knife
- 4 kaffir lime leaves (or zest of 1 lime as substitute)
- 1 inch fresh ginger, sliced
- 1 can (400ml) full-fat coconut milk
- ½ cup coconut cream
- 3 tbsp fish sauce
- 2½ tbsp fresh lime juice
- 1 tsp sugar
- Salt to taste

### Method
Melt butter in a saucepan over medium-low. Add leeks with a pinch of salt and sweat slowly 8–10 minutes until completely soft and silky — no browning. Add lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and ginger, stir 1 minute. Pour in coconut milk and coconut cream. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook uncovered 20 minutes.

Strain through a fine-mesh sieve, pressing down on solids. Return liquid to pan and season with fish sauce, lime juice, and sugar. Taste critically — the sauce should have a clear acidic brightness cutting through the coconut richness. Adjust lime and fish sauce as needed. The sauce should coat a spoon but still pour easily. Keep warm.

---

## Cilantro, Thai Basil & Mint Vinaigrette

### Ingredients
- 1 large bunch cilantro, leaves and tender stems
- Small handful Thai basil leaves
- Small handful mint leaves
- ½ inch fresh ginger, peeled and roughly chopped
- 2 tbsp fresh lime juice
- 1 tbsp rice wine vinegar
- 1 tsp honey
- 1 tbsp fish sauce
- ¼ cup neutral oil
- Salt to taste

### Method
Blanch cilantro, Thai basil, and mint together in boiling salted water for 30 seconds, then immediately shock in ice water. Squeeze completely dry — this is essential for a vivid green colour.

Blend the blanched herbs with ginger, lime juice, rice vinegar, honey, and fish sauce until smooth. With the blender running, drizzle in oil. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve for a clean finish. Season with salt. The consistency should be pourable but thick enough to hold a dollop shape on the plate.

---

## Seared Diver Scallops

### Ingredients
- 12 large diver scallops (U10 or U12)
- Kosher salt
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 2 tbsp unsalted butter
- Flaky sea salt (Maldon) for finishing

### Method
30 minutes before cooking, season scallops lightly with kosher salt and let sit uncovered — this draws out surface moisture. Right before cooking, pat completely dry with paper towels.

Get a heavy stainless or cast iron pan ripping hot. Add oil. When it begins to smoke, lay scallops flat side down without crowding. Do not move them. Sear 1½–2 minutes until a deep mahogany crust forms. Flip, add butter, and baste continuously for 1 minute. The centre should still be slightly translucent when pulled. Rest 1 minute, finish with flaky salt.

# Optional Enhancements

---

## Yuzu Gel Dots

*The visual and acidic element of the dish — bright, glossy, and intensely citrusy without any heat.*

### Ingredients
- 100ml yuzu juice (bottled is fine)
- 50ml water
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1.5g agar agar powder

### Method
Combine yuzu juice, water, sugar, and agar agar in a small saucepan. Bring to a boil, whisking, and simmer 2 minutes. Pour into a shallow tray to set a layer about 5mm deep. Refrigerate until firmly set, at least 1 hour.

Once set, cut into small cubes (roughly 5mm) with a sharp knife. Keep refrigerated until plating. They should be translucent, pale yellow-green, and hold their shape on the plate while bursting with acid when eaten.

---

## Pickled Watermelon Radish

*For extra visual drama — thin rounds of vivid magenta alongside the yuzu gel make the plate genuinely striking.*

### Ingredients
- 1 watermelon radish, peeled and sliced paper-thin on a mandoline
- 100ml rice wine vinegar
- 100ml water
- 1 tbsp sugar
- 1 tsp kosher salt

### Method
Bring vinegar, water, sugar, and salt to a simmer, stirring until dissolved. Pour over radish slices in a jar or bowl. Cool to room temperature, then refrigerate at least 1 hour. Will keep for a week. Fan 3–4 thin rounds per plate at plating.

---

## Fried Shallots

### Ingredients
- 4 large shallots, sliced paper-thin on a mandoline
- ½ cup cornstarch
- Neutral oil for frying
- Salt

### Method
Separate the rings and toss in cornstarch, shaking off any excess. Fry in 325°F (165°C) oil until pale golden — they continue darkening after removal. Drain on a wire rack and season immediately with salt.

---

## Optional: Coconut-Braised Pork Belly

*A richer, more substantial version of the dish. The braise echoes the flavours of the coconut sauce, making the belly feel like a natural part of the dish rather than an addition.*

### Ingredients
- 400g pork belly, skin on, cut into 4 equal portions
- 1 can (400ml) full-fat coconut milk
- 1 stalk lemongrass, bruised
- 2 kaffir lime leaves
- 1 inch ginger, sliced
- 2 star anise
- 2 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp palm sugar or brown sugar
- Water to cover

### Method
Season pork belly portions with salt. Sear skin-side down in a hot ovenproof pan until golden. Flip and sear the underside briefly. Add coconut milk, lemongrass, kaffir lime, ginger, star anise, fish sauce, and sugar. Top up with water to just cover. Bring to a simmer, cover, and braise in a 160°C (320°F) oven for 2–2.5 hours until completely tender.

Remove from braising liquid and press between two trays in the fridge overnight. Before service, portion and sear skin-side down in a very hot dry pan until crispy and deeply caramelised. Place one piece per plate alongside or in place of some of the rice cakes.

### Alternative Proteins

- **King prawns** — Split and sear cut-side down in butter. Fast, sweet, and an obvious match with coconut and lime.
- **Duck breast** — Score the skin, render slowly then finish in the oven. Rest well. The richness of duck against the acidic coconut sauce is excellent. A touch of five spice in the seasoning bridges the flavours.
- **Pan-seared sea bass or halibut** — A fillet seared skin-side until crispy, flesh barely cooked through. Lighter and more delicate — a good choice if the dish is already carrying the pork belly.

---

## Assembly

### Base dish
1. Warm the coconut velouté and spoon a generous pool into the centre of each bowl (about ¼ cup per portion).
2. Arrange 2–3 rice cakes and 3 scallops per plate directly on the sauce, allowing the rice cakes to begin soaking.
3. Add 5–6 small spoonfuls of the herb vinaigrette scattered around the plate.
4. Pile fried shallots on top of the scallops.
5. Microplane fresh lime zest over the entire dish just before serving.

### With optional enhancements
- Dot yuzu gel cubes around the plate — 6–8 pieces per portion.
- Fan 3–4 pickled watermelon radish rounds at the edge of the sauce pool.
- Place one piece of pork belly per plate, skin-side up, alongside the scallops.
