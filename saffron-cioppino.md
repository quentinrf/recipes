# Saffron Cioppino with Aioli Crostini

*A tomato-based seafood stew with bloomed saffron, served with garlic aioli crostini and fresh parsley*

**Serves:** 4
**Total Time:** ~1 hour

---

## Ingredients

### Stew — Base

- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 1 fennel bulb, thinly sliced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp chili flakes
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 generous pinch saffron, bloomed in ¼ cup warm water
- 1 cup dry white wine (Pinot Grigio or Sauvignon Blanc)
- 1 can (796ml) crushed San Marzano tomatoes
- 2 cups fish or seafood stock
- Salt & pepper to taste

### Stew — Seafood

- 500g clams, cleaned
- 500g mussels, cleaned and debearded
- 300g shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 300g firm white fish (cod, halibut, or sea bass), cut into chunks
- 200g scallops or squid rings *(optional)*

### Stew — Finish

- 1 tbsp butter
- Small handful fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped

### Aioli

- 2 egg yolks, room temperature
- 2 cloves garlic, finely minced or grated
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tbsp lemon juice, plus more to taste
- ½ cup neutral oil (grapeseed or light olive oil)
- ¼ cup good quality extra-virgin olive oil
- Salt to taste

### Crostini

- 1 baguette, cut on the diagonal into ½-inch slices
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Flaky salt

---

## Method

### 1. Bloom the Saffron

Place a generous pinch of saffron threads in ¼ cup warm (not boiling) water. Let steep for at least 10 minutes. The water should turn a deep golden colour. Set aside.

### 2. Make the Aioli

Whisk egg yolks, garlic, Dijon, and lemon juice together in a bowl. Very slowly drizzle in the neutral oil while whisking constantly — a few drops at a time at first — until emulsified. Once thick and stable, whisk in the olive oil. Season with salt and more lemon to taste. Refrigerate until ready to use.

### 3. Make the Crostini

Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Arrange baguette slices in a single layer on a baking sheet, cut on the diagonal. Brush with olive oil and sprinkle lightly with flaky salt. Bake for 10–12 minutes until golden and crisp at the edges but still slightly soft in the centre. Set aside.

### 4. Build the Stew Base

Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium heat. Sauté onion and fennel for 6–8 minutes until soft and just beginning to caramelise. Add garlic and chili flakes, cook 1–2 minutes until fragrant.

### 5. Deglaze & Develop the Broth

Pour in the white wine and let it reduce by half, about 3 minutes. Add the crushed tomatoes, stock, oregano, and the bloomed saffron with its soaking liquid. Stir to combine. Bring to a simmer and cook uncovered for 15–20 minutes, allowing the broth to deepen in flavour and take on the saffron's golden hue. Taste and season well with salt and pepper — the broth should be bold before the seafood goes in.

### 6. Add the Seafood in Stages

Add seafood in order of cooking time — do not rush this step:

1. **Clams & mussels** — add first, cover the pot, cook 2 minutes.
2. **Fish chunks** — add after 2 minutes, they need 4–5 minutes total.
3. **Shrimp & scallops** — add in the final 2–3 minutes. They overcook quickly.

Once all shellfish have opened and shrimp are pink and just opaque, remove from heat. Stir in butter and half the parsley.

### 7. Assemble & Serve

Ladle the stew into deep bowls. Generously slather each crostini with aioli and rest a few on top of the stew, aioli-side up. Finish with the remaining fresh parsley. Serve immediately with extra crostini on the side.

---

## Tips

- **Don't overcook the seafood** — it continues cooking in the residual heat of the broth even off the heat. Pull it slightly early.
- **Saffron quality matters** — look for deep red threads with a strong aroma. A little goes a long way.
- **Taste the broth before adding seafood** — once the seafood is in, there's no time to adjust seasoning.
- **San Marzano tomatoes** make a noticeable difference in sweetness and depth over generic canned tomatoes.
- **Aioli ahead of time** — the aioli can be made up to 2 days in advance and kept covered in the fridge.
- **Discard any shellfish** that don't open during cooking.

---

## Wine Pairing

A crisp, mineral white works beautifully — the same Pinot Grigio or Sauvignon Blanc used in the stew is an easy choice. A Provençal rosé or a white Burgundy (Mâcon-Villages) are equally at home with the saffron and fennel notes.
